Canvas apps are a type of app in Microsoft Power Apps that let users build custom business applications on a blank canvas using drag‑and‑drop controls and Excel‑like formulas, without writing traditional code.



Concise summary

- Canvas apps in Power Apps allow you to build business apps without coding, using a blank canvas and Excel-like expressions.
- You can create apps with Copilot by describing what you want the app to do in natural language, and AI handles the rest, or automatically generate an app from various sources like Dataverse, SharePoint, or Excel.
- Once created, apps can be customized, shared with others in your organization, and run on various devices, including Windows, web browsers, iOS, or Android devices.


Detailed summary

- Canvas apps in Power Apps provide a platform for building business apps without requiring coding skills, allowing users to drag components and design a user interface in any layout they want, and use Excel-like expressions to specify logic and work with data from various Microsoft and third-party sources.
- The Power Apps platform enables users to share their apps so that others can run them in a browser or on a mobile device, and also allows embedding apps in SharePoint, Power BI, or Teams for seamless integration.
- With Copilot in Power Apps, users can create apps by describing what they want the app to do in natural language, and the AI handles the rest, providing a range of articles and resources to help build apps in different scenarios, including using the Plan designer, building apps through conversation with Copilot, and editing apps with Copilot in Power Apps Studio.
- Users can automatically generate an app from various sources, such as a sample app, a template, Dataverse, SharePoint, or Excel, and then customize the app's default appearance and behavior based on their users' workflows, including changing data types, sorting, and adding or customizing screens, galleries, forms, and other controls.
- After generating and customizing an app, users can create an app from scratch based on Dataverse, Excel, or another data source, providing flexibility in app design, flow, and controls, and allowing the use of a larger variety of data sources.
- For new users, the platform provides resources such as Planning a Power Apps project, and also offers options for users without a license, including signing up for free or establishing a developer's account through the Microsoft Developer Program.
- Once an app is finished and saved to the cloud, users can share it with others in their organization, specifying which users or groups can run the app and whether they can customize and share it with more people, and apps can be run on Windows, in a web browser, or on an iOS or Android device.
- The Power Apps platform also provides additional resources, including step-by-step, conceptual, and reference articles, webinars, and video galleries, as well as a community forum where users can post questions and ideas, and submit support tickets in the Power Platform admin center for administrators.
